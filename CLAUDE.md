# disPathCuration

## Writing style

Applies to all prose written for this project: README, docstrings, comments, reports,
commit messages, and answers in chat.

- Be concise. Cut adjectives that carry no information. Prefer one clear sentence over two
  hedged ones.
- Be scientific. State what is done, what is assumed, and what is unknown. Do not present
  an untested hypothesis as a result. Name the data source behind a claim.
- Never use em dashes or en dashes in prose. Use a full stop, a colon, a semicolon, a
  comma, or parentheses instead. In compound terms use a hyphen, for example
  "target-disease evidence".
- Avoid marketing register: no "powerful", "seamless", "comes for free", "unlock".
- Use British spelling, consistent with EMBL-EBI convention.
